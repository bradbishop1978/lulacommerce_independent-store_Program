Dashboard A — Store Signing Progress

What it's for

This is your weekly tracker for the Q2 goal: 107 stores signed in 10 weeks starting April 1, 2026. It tells you whether you're on pace, ahead, or falling behind — and when you'll hit the goal at your current rate.

How to enter data

1
Scroll to the Add Store Signings form at the bottom of the page.
2
Set the date of the signing (when the store committed, not when you filed the paperwork).
3
Choose the brand from the dropdown.
4
Enter the number of stores signed in that batch (you can log multiple stores at once).
5
Add optional notes (market, rep name, anything useful). Click + Add Entry.
Tip: Log entries by the actual signing date, not today's date. The "current pace" metric looks at the last 7 calendar days, so accurate dates give you accurate pace readings.

How to read the dashboard

Progress bar
% of 107 stores signed. The bar turns pink as you close in.
Status card
Green = on track (≥10.7/wk), Yellow = at risk (8–10.6/wk), Red = behind (<8/wk).
Current Pace
Stores signed in the last 7 days. This is what the status color is based on.
Required Pace
Stores/week needed from today to hit 107 by the end of week 10.
Projection
The plain-English sentence tells you exactly when you'll hit 107 at current pace — and how late (or early) that is.
Cumulative line chart
Your actual progress (pink) vs the required pace line (gray dashes). If you're above the gray line, you're ahead.

Managing your data

All data is stored in your browser (localStorage). It stays between sessions but is specific to this browser and device. Use Export CSV to back up your entries or share them. Use Import CSV to restore from a backup or load entries from a spreadsheet.

Dashboard B — Call Activity Tracker

Getting started

Open the dashboard — it connects to Airtable automatically and loads all call records. The first load fetches everything fresh. Subsequent visits use cached data instantly and refresh in the background.

Hit Refresh in the top-right corner any time you want to pull the latest records from Airtable mid-session.

Using filters

The filter bar at the top lets you slice the data before anything is computed. All KPIs, charts, and the table respond immediately — no page reload needed.

Brand Filter by one or more brands (KKC, Circle K, etc.)
Date Range Default is the last 7 days. Set a wider range for trend analysis.
Call Status Filter by Completed, No Answer, Voicemail, etc.
End Result Filter to specific outcomes (e.g. only show "Callback Scheduled" calls).

Click Clear Filters to reset everything back to the default 7-day view.

How to read the KPIs

Volume row
Total calls, calls today, this week, and average call duration.
Reach row
How often SimOne gets through to the actual decision-maker and captures their info.
Outcomes row
Follow-up rate, voicemail rate, and SMS/email permission rates (calculated against answered calls only).
Interest level bar
Breakdown of email interest from Airtable: high/medium/low/none. More green = stronger pipeline.
Top End Results
The most common call outcomes, ranked by frequency. Quick snapshot of what's happening on calls.
Callback Scheduled
Count of records with a specific callback time set — your warm pipeline for the next batch.

Conversion funnel

The funnel shows how calls move through the pipeline. Each stage narrows as fewer records qualify:

Total Calls Dialed → Calls Connected (completed/answered) → Reached Decision Maker → Expressed Interest (follow-up or high interest) → Callback Scheduled → Store Signed

The percentage on the right of each stage shows the conversion rate from the previous step — not from total. A low DM-to-interest rate means the pitch is where to focus. A low connected-to-DM rate means gatekeeper handling.

Charts

Daily Volume
Call volume per day for the last 14 days. Good for spotting gaps in outreach cadence.
End Result Distribution
Donut chart of all outcome types. Shows the mix of voicemails, no-answers, callbacks, etc.
Primary Objections
Most common objections SimOne logged. Informs which objection scripts need refinement.
Brand Performance
Calls, DM reach, and follow-ups per brand. Useful for comparing brand-level results.
Duration by Outcome
Average call length per end result. Longer calls on signed/callback outcomes is a healthy sign.

Reading the charts — what they tell you and what to do about it

End Result Distribution

This donut chart shows the proportion of each outcome type across all filtered calls. Its value is in spotting domination — when one outcome is eating an outsized share of your calls.

What to look for: If 70%+ of the donut is "No Answer," SimOne isn't getting through — the problem is reach (wrong time of day, wrong number type). If 40%+ is "Voicemail," timing is the issue — consider shifting call windows to mid-morning or post-lunch. If "Callback Scheduled" or "Signed" has a visible slice, that's your pipeline working. The goal is to grow those slices at the expense of the No Answer and Voicemail slices.

This chart lives alongside Top End Results — the donut gives you the visual weight of each outcome (what's dominating), while Top End Results gives you the raw counts and the exact Airtable labels.

Top End Results

Shows the five most frequent outcomes as logged verbatim in Airtable by SimOne. Unlike the donut, this is your data integrity check — if you see inconsistent capitalizations ("No answer" vs "No Answer") or unexpected values you don't recognize, that's a sign SimOne's outcome classification needs review.

What to do: The labels here should be clean and consistent. If you see more than one pill representing the same real outcome (e.g., "Voicemail" and "voicemail"), the underlying VAPI analysis plan needs to enforce a fixed enum. Each unique label is its own slice in the donut — messy labels artificially fragment the chart.

Brand Performance

Compares brands side by side across three metrics: calls made, decision-maker reach rate, and follow-up scheduled rate. Volume alone doesn't tell you which brand is working — this chart shows which brand converts best per call.

What to do: If one brand has high call volume but low DM reach, SimOne is hitting the wrong contacts or getting stuck at gatekeepers for that brand. If DM reach is strong but follow-up rate is low, the pitch for that brand needs refinement. The brand with the strongest combined DM + follow-up rate is where you double outreach — more calls there, not spread evenly.

Conversion Funnel

This is the most diagnostic view in the dashboard. It doesn't just show you how many calls succeeded — it shows you exactly where calls are breaking down. Each stage shows its conversion rate from the previous step (not from total).

How to read the drop-offs:
— Low Connected → DM rate: Gatekeeper problem. SimOne is connecting but not getting transferred. Focus on the gatekeeper script.
— Low DM → Interest rate: Pitch problem. The decision maker is hearing SimOne but not engaging. Review the discovery questions and opening.
— Low Interest → Callback rate: Follow-up process problem. Interest is there but not converting to a scheduled next step. Review how SimOne closes State 7.
— Low Callback → Signed rate: Human follow-through gap. The warm pipeline exists but isn't being worked. This is a rep action item, not a SimOne fix.

Call log table

Shows every filtered call record — 25 rows per page. Click any column header to sort. Click any row to open a detail panel on the right with every field for that call, including the full call summary that SimOne logged.

Click Export CSV to download all filtered records (not just the current page).

Using Both Dashboards Together

Dashboard A answers: "Are we signing stores fast enough to hit the goal?"
Dashboard B answers: "How are the calls performing, and where is SimOne in the funnel?"

Weekly review workflow: Open Dashboard B first — check the funnel conversion and objection chart to see where calls are stalling. Then open Dashboard A to check if the signing pace is on track. If calls are converting but signing pace is lagging, the gap is in the follow-up handoff. If calls aren't connecting, the issue is reach.
